# Yunara ADC — Optimal Build from First Principles

## Champion Profile (Patch 16.5.1)

### Base Stats
- AD: 55 + 2.5/lvl | AS: 0.65 base + 2%/lvl | Range: 575 | MS: 325
- HP: 590 + 110/lvl | Armor: 25 + 4.4/lvl | MR: 30 + 1.3/lvl
- Mana: 275 + 45/lvl | Crit Multiplier: 200% (standard)
- Secondary Resource: Petals/Seeds (max 8, generated by hitting things)

### Ability Ratios (Exact from CommunityDragon)

**Passive — Blossoming Burst**
- Crit damage amplifier: 10% + 0.1% per AP (displayed as percent)
- This means crits deal bonus magic damage scaled to your AP
- At 0 AP: crits deal 10% bonus magic damage
- At 100 AP: crits deal 20% bonus magic damage
- The passive itself can roll for crit (`mRollForCriticalHit: true`)
- Key insight: AP gives a nice bonus but the base 10% is already there at 0 AP — you don't NEED AP items to benefit

**Q — (Toggle, 30 mana, no cooldown)**
- **Active on-hit**: 5/10/15/20/25 + 20% AP magic damage per auto
- **Passive on-hit**: 5/10/15/20/25 + 20% AP magic damage per auto
- **Combined on-hit**: 10/20/30/40/50 + 40% AP magic damage
- **Attack Speed bonus while active**: 20%/30%/40%/50%/60%
- **Spread Attack**: 30% total AD physical damage, AoE (300 radius)
  - CAN CRIT (confirmed: `mRollForCriticalHit: true`)
  - Applies on-hit effects at 30% efficacy
  - 100% lifesteal efficacy
  - Missile speed: 2000
- **Minion execute**: Below 30% HP, deals 250% damage
- **Resource**: Generates petals (2 per champion hit, 1 per non-champ). Petals decay after 6s at 1 per 0.5s.

**W — (Skillshot, 60 mana, no explicit cooldown in data — likely charge-based)**
- **Initial damage**: 55/95/135/175/215 + 85% bonus AD + 50% AP
- **Lingering zone DPS**: 60% of initial damage per second for 1.0s
- **CC**: 99% slow for 1.5 seconds
- **Range**: 1150 | Width: 60 | Missile speed: 2150
- **Monster damage modifier**: 50% (lvl 1-8), 75% (lvl 9-12), 100% (lvl 13+)

**E — (Self-cast MS buff, 40 mana, 9s cooldown)**
- **Move Speed**: 30%/35%/40%/45%/50% for 1.5 seconds
- **Enhanced (during R)**: 1.5× multiplier → 45%/52.5%/60%/67.5%/75% MS
- **During R**: Becomes a DASH (range 450, speed 1350/1500/1650 by R rank)

**R — (Self-cast transformation, 100 mana, 100/90/80s CD)**
- **Duration**: 15 seconds
- **Empowered W (R-W) damage**: 160/320/480 + 120% bonus AD + 75% AP
- **R-W CC**: 99% slow for 1.0s (shorter than base W's 1.5s)
- **R-W CDR**: 80% cooldown reduction on W during R
- **E becomes a dash** during R (see E above)
- **Note**: R itself doesn't explicitly state "+50% AS" or "guaranteed crits" in the data values — these may be script-driven or part of the Q interaction during R

### Runaan's Hurricane Special Interaction (CONFIRMED)
Yunara has 4 custom Hurricane bolt objects in her data:
- `ItemHurricaneYunaraAttack` — normal AA bolts (speed 2500)
- `ItemHurricaneYunaraAttackCrit` — crit AA bolts (speed 2500)
- `ItemHurricaneYunaraQAttack` — Q-active bolts (speed 10000)
- `ItemHurricaneYunaraQAttackCrit` — Q-active crit bolts (speed 10000)

Each Hurricane bolt triggers its own Q spread attack. In teamfights: primary auto + 2 bolts = 3 spread attacks, each dealing 30% AD AoE that can crit.

## Stat Priority

**Crit >> AD ≥ AS >> Armor Pen > AP (incidental)**

1. **Crit** — Passive adds magic damage to every crit. Q spread can crit. R mechanics involve crits. Crit is THE stat.
2. **AD** — Q spread scales with 30% total AD. W scales with 85% bonus AD. R-W scales with 120% bonus AD. Autos scale with AD.
3. **AS** — Q gives 20-60% AS already. More AS = more crits, more Q on-hits, more spread attacks. But Q's built-in AS reduces the marginal value of AS items.
4. **Armor Pen** — Late game when everyone has armor. Essential for physical damage to cut through.
5. **AP** — Q on-hit scales 40% AP, passive scales 0.1% per AP. Nice but AP items don't give crit/AD/AS, so it's a rider stat, not something you itemize for.

## Item-by-Item Evaluation

### CRITICAL DISCOVERY: Patch 16.5.1 Item Changes

Several items have changed significantly from what you might expect:
- **Kraken Slayer**: NO CRIT. It's 45 AD + 40% AS + 4% MS + on-hit passive. Still good but doesn't contribute to crit scaling.
- **Yun Tal Wildarrows**: STARTS AT 0% CRIT, ramps to 25% via stacking passive. 50 AD + 40% AS + Flurry (30% AS for 6s on attacking champions).
- **Bloodthirster**: NO CRIT. 80 AD + 15% lifesteal. Pure sustain item.
- **Statikk Shiv**: NO CRIT. 45 AD + 30% AS + chain lightning.
- **IE**: 75 AD + 25% crit + 30% crit damage. No threshold requirement mentioned — pure stat stick.
- **Phantom Dancer**: 65% AS + 25% crit + 10% MS + Ghosted. Highest AS/MS of any Zeal item.

This changes the build significantly from what the old doc assumed.

### First Item Candidates

#### Yun Tal Wildarrows (3100g) — 50 AD, 40% AS, ramps to 25% crit
- **Practice Makes Lethal**: On-attack, gain crit permanently up to 25%. You START stacking from first auto.
- **Flurry**: Attacking a champion gives 30% AS for 6s (30s CD, reduced by autos — 1s per auto, 2s per crit).
- **Why it's the best first item for Yunara**:
  - 50 AD feeds Q spread (30% total AD) and W (85% bonus AD)
  - 40% AS base + Flurry's 30% AS on champion combat = 70% bonus AS during fights. Combined with Q's 20-60% AS bonus, you're hitting extremely fast.
  - The 25% crit ramp means you're stacking toward passive procs through the game
  - Flurry's CD reduces by 2s per crit — once you have crit, Flurry is up constantly
- **Build path**: BF Sword (1300g) + Scout's Slingshot (600g) + Long Sword (350g) + 850g
- **The BF Sword problem**: 1300g first back is demanding. But BF Sword gives the biggest single-component AD spike in the game (40 AD). If you get it, your lane pressure is enormous.
- **If you can't afford BF Sword**: Buy Long Sword (350g) + Scout's Slingshot components (Daggers, 250g each). The build path has cheap components too.

#### Kraken Slayer (3000g) — 45 AD, 40% AS, 4% MS, on-hit
- **Bring It Down**: Every 3rd auto deals bonus physical damage, increased by missing HP.
- **Why it's still viable**: Smooth build path — Rectrix (775g), Hearthbound Axe (1200g), Recurve Bow (700g). You can always buy a component.
- **Why Yun Tal is better for Yunara specifically**:
  - Kraken gives NO CRIT. Yunara's passive, Q spread crits, and R mechanics all scale with crit. Zero crit from your first item delays your scaling.
  - 5 less AD than Yun Tal.
  - Kraken's on-hit is generic. Yun Tal's Flurry gives 30% AS in champion fights, which is far more AS than Kraken's 4% MS.
  - Yun Tal ramps to 25% crit, which starts activating passive procs.
- **When Kraken is better**: If you're in a lane where you can't get 1300g first back AND you need to fight constantly (the on-hit damage from Bring It Down is stronger in short trades than Yun Tal before crit ramps up).

#### Immortal Shieldbow (3000g) — 55 AD, 25% crit, Lifeline shield
- **Why consider it**: 55 AD + 25% crit is actually great stats for Yunara. The Lifeline shield saves you from burst.
- **When to build it first**: Against heavy burst lanes (Draven + Leona, or if enemy jungle is a gank-heavy assassin). The shield can be the difference between dying and getting R off.
- **Build path**: Pickaxe (875g) + Noonquiver (1300g — Long Sword + Cloak of Agility). Noonquiver gives crit, which is great.
- **Downside**: No AS. Yunara wants AS from items because even though Q gives AS, you need a baseline to make Q feel smooth before it's ranked up.

### FIRST ITEM VERDICT

**Default: Kraken Slayer** — Smooth build path wins in practice. Hearthbound Axe (1200g) is a realistic, strong first-back spike. Rectrix at 775g and Recurve Bow at 700g mean you can ALWAYS buy a useful component regardless of back timing. The on-hit from Bring It Down gives real lane pressure. You don't need to gamble on a 1300g first back.

**Upgrade: Yun Tal Wildarrows** — When you back with 1300g+ for BF Sword. The finished item is better for Yunara (crit ramp + Flurry AS burst), but the build path demands that big first buy. If you get BF Sword, your lane pressure is enormous and the crit scaling kicks in faster.

**Defensive: Immortal Shieldbow** — Against burst-heavy lanes/games where you need to survive to scale.

## Defensive Itemization — vs Assassins

This is the section you asked about. The question is: **what do you buy and WHEN do you buy it when an assassin is killing you?**

### The Core Problem

ADC itemization is a razor's edge. Every slot spent on defense is DPS you don't have. But dead ADCs deal zero damage. The art is knowing:
1. Which defensive item gives the most survival per gold/slot spent
2. At what point in the build to buy it
3. Whether the defensive item component (Stopwatch, Hexdrinker, etc.) is enough or you need the full item

### Defensive Item Evaluation

#### Guardian Angel (3200g) — 55 AD, 45 Armor, Revive
- **Rebirth**: On death, revive with 50% base HP after 4s stasis (300s CD)
- **The problem with GA vs assassins**:
  - You have to DIE first. Against Zed or Rengar, they burst you, you die, you revive with 50% HP in a predictable spot — and they (or their team) just kill you again. The revive location is known and you come back low HP.
  - 55 AD is real damage and 45 Armor helps, but the passive is a delayed death more often than a save against dedicated assassins.
- **When GA IS good**: Against general teamfight threats where your team can protect your revive location. If nobody is specifically diving you but you might die to stray AoE or get caught, GA is a good safety net.
- **Build path**: Steel Sigil (1100g — Cloth Armor + Long Sword + Cloth Armor, gives some armor early) + BF Sword (1300g) + 800g
- **When to buy**: 4th or 5th item when there's no specific assassin threat but you want insurance.
- **Key component**: Steel Sigil at 1100g gives 25 Armor and can be sat on while you complete your core.

#### Immortal Shieldbow (3000g) — 55 AD, 25% crit, Lifeline shield
- **Lifeline**: Below 30% HP, grants a shield for 3s.
- **Why it's good on Yunara**: 55 AD + 25% crit — it's actually an offensive item that also saves you. The crit contributes to your passive.
- **When to buy**: Against burst that happens too fast for you to react (Rengar one-shot from stealth, Zed R combo). The shield is automatic — you don't need to press anything.
- **Shares Lifeline with Maw and Sterak's** — only one can proc. Don't stack these.
- **Can be first item** in extreme cases (see First Item section above).
- **Best timing**: 4th item, replacing Runaan's or LDR slot.

#### Zhonya's Hourglass (3250g) — 105 AP, 50 Armor, Stasis active
- **Time Stop**: 2.5s stasis on demand.
- **Why Zhonya's is the BEST anti-assassin item for Yunara**:
  - **You press it BEFORE you die.** This is the key difference vs GA. Zed ults you → stasis → his Death Mark pops for zero damage. Rengar leaps → you stasis → he's standing in the middle of your team with no target and no cooldowns. You come OUT of stasis alive, at full HP, with your team collapsed on the assassin.
  - **50 Armor** — more than GA's 45. The most armor of any single item.
  - **105 AP is NOT wasted on Yunara**:
    - Q on-hit: 40% AP = +42 magic damage per auto
    - Passive: 10% + 10.5% = 20.5% bonus magic damage on crits (vs 10% at 0 AP)
    - W: 50% AP = +52.5 damage
    - R-W: 75% AP = +78.75 damage
    - This adds meaningful MIXED damage that's harder for enemies to itemize against
  - **Controllable timing**: Unlike Shieldbow's auto-shield or GA's death trigger, YOU choose when to stasis. This lets you outplay specific abilities (Zed R, Karthus R, Fizz R).
  - **Seeker's Armguard (1600g)** in the build path gives 40 AP + 25 Armor + a single-use stasis. You get defensive value while building toward the completed item.
- **The cost**: No AD, no crit, no AS from this slot. Your auto-attack DPS doesn't scale. But dead ADCs deal zero damage — if Zhonya's keeps you alive to auto for 5 more seconds, it outdamages any offensive item.
- **When to buy**: 4th-5th item against Zed, Rengar, Talon, Kha'Zix, or any AD assassin that commits hard onto you. Can sit on Seeker's Armguard (1600g) earlier if you're getting dove.
- **Build path note**: NLR (1200g) feels bad on an ADC. You can go Seeker's first (1600g — armor + stasis immediately useful) and finish Zhonya's later.

#### Maw of Malmortius (3100g) — 60 AD, 15 AH, 40 MR, Lifeline (magic shield)
- **When**: Against AP assassins (Evelynn, AP Shaco, LeBlanc, Fizz, Katarina).
- 60 AD is good damage. 40 MR + magic shield is strong vs magic burst.
- **Shares Lifeline** — don't stack with Shieldbow.
- **Verdict**: Buy 4th-5th item when AP burst is the threat, not AD assassins.

#### Wit's End (2800g) — 50% AS, 45 MR, 20% Tenacity, on-hit magic damage
- **When**: Against AP-heavy teams where you need MR + want DPS.
- 50% AS is massive. Tenacity helps vs CC chains.
- No crit, no AD. Doesn't synergize with Yunara's crit scaling.
- **Verdict**: Niche pick vs heavy AP + CC comps. PD + Maw usually better.

#### Death's Dance (3300g) — 60 AD, 50 Armor, 15 AH, damage delay + cleanse
- **Ignore Pain**: 30% of damage taken becomes a bleed over 3s instead of instant damage.
- **Defy**: On takedown (within 3s of damaging), cleanse ALL pending bleed + heal over 2s.
- **Why Death's Dance is the BEST anti-bruiser/fighter item for Yunara**:
  - Bruisers (Jax, Irelia, Riven, Darius, Camille) don't one-shot — they run you down over 3-5 seconds. Death's Dance converts their damage into a manageable bleed while you kite with E (30-50% MS) and W (99% slow). You have TIME to DPS them.
  - If the bruiser dies (or your team kills them), the pending bleed vanishes entirely via Defy AND you heal. In a teamfight where you're kiting the frontline diver, this means you effectively took 30% less total damage.
  - **60 AD is the highest AD of any defensive item**. Real damage contribution:
    - Q spread: 30% × 60 = +18 per spread attack
    - W: 85% × 60 = +51 damage
    - R-W: 120% × 60 = +72 damage
    - Auto attacks: +60 per auto (amplified by crit)
  - 50 Armor (tied with Zhonya's for most armor on any item, more than GA's 45)
  - 15 AH — the ONLY defensive option that gives AH. Reduces W cooldown for more slows, E for more MS bursts while kiting.
- **Death's Dance vs Zhonya's**:
  - vs Assassins (instant burst): Zhonya's wins. DD delays 30% but you still eat 70% instantly — if the one-shot is 2000 damage, you take 1400 up front. Probably still dead. Stasis prevents ALL of it.
  - vs Bruisers/fighters (sustained dive): DD wins. Bruisers don't one-shot. DD gives you time to kite + DPS them, and Defy cleanses the bleed if they die. Zhonya's stasis just delays the inevitable — you come out and the bruiser is still on top of you.
- **When to buy**: 4th-5th item when the primary threat is bruisers diving your backline, not assassins bursting you. Especially good when your team can help collapse on the diver (Defy procs on ANY takedown you participated in, not just solo kills).
- **Build path**: Steel Sigil (1100g — armor early) + Pickaxe (875g — AD) + Caulfield's Warhammer (1050g — AD + AH) + 275g. Very smooth — every component is useful.

#### Mercurial Scimitar (3200g) — 50 AD, 35 MR, 10% Lifesteal, QSS active
- **When**: Against Malzahar R, Skarner R, Mordekaiser R, or heavy CC that your support can't peel.
- The QSS active removes CC — if the assassin's kill combo requires CC (Zed R is just damage, not CC — so QSS doesn't help vs Zed).
- **Verdict**: Only buy this if there's a specific CC ability that you MUST cleanse to survive. Not a general anti-assassin item.

## Build Order by Game State

### Standard (no assassin threat)
```
Yun Tal (3100g) → Berserker's (1100g) → Fiendhunter Bolts (2650g)
→ Infinity Edge (3500g) → Runaan's Hurricane (2650g) → LDR (3300g)
```
100% crit at 4 items. Maximum teamfight AoE with Runaan's. LDR for late-game armor pen.

### vs AD Assassin (Shaco, Zed, Talon, Kha'Zix, Rengar)

**If the assassin is NOT fed (0-1 kills, behind or even):**
```
Yun Tal → Berserker's → Fiendhunter Bolts → IE → GA → LDR/Runaan's
```
Standard build, GA 4th. You have enough damage to kill them if they engage. GA ensures you survive the all-in.

**If the assassin is fed (2+ kills, actively ganking you):**
```
Yun Tal → Steelcaps → Fiendhunter Bolts → IE → GA → LDR
```
Steelcaps instead of Berserker's. You lose 25% AS but gain 25 Armor + 10% auto damage reduction. Against a Shaco who backstabs for 500 damage, that 10% reduction saves 50 damage per auto — and the armor reduces the rest.

**If the assassin is HYPER fed (4+ kills, one-shotting you):**
```
Yun Tal → Steelcaps → Shieldbow → Fiendhunter Bolts → IE → GA
```
Shieldbow second item. You delay your damage spike significantly, but you don't die. Shieldbow gives 55 AD + 25% crit, so it's not pure defense — it still contributes to your scaling. Once you add Fiendhunter + IE, you still hit the 75% crit threshold.

**If AD Shaco specifically:**
- Shaco's main threat is backstab burst from stealth + clone confusion.
- **Key counterplay**: Shroom-like awareness (pink wards, sweeper) + the GA revive wastes his entire combo.
- Build: Standard with GA 4th. Steelcaps if he's ahead.
- **Unique consideration**: Shaco's clone deals reduced damage. If he ults, you can often just auto the real one down — your sustained DPS with crit items will out-trade a Shaco who blew his burst. GA ensures you survive the initial burst to start auto-attacking.

### vs AP Assassin (Evelynn, LeBlanc, Fizz, Katarina, AP Shaco)
```
Yun Tal → Berserker's → Fiendhunter Bolts → IE → Maw of Malmortius → LDR
```
Maw 4th. 60 AD + 40 MR + magic damage shield. You keep damage scaling while gaining magic burst protection. Don't buy Shieldbow if you're buying Maw — they share Lifeline.

### vs Heavy Dive (multiple divers, not just assassin)
```
Yun Tal → Berserker's → Fiendhunter Bolts → IE → PD → GA
```
PD 4th for 10% MS + Ghosted (can't be body-blocked). GA 5th for the revive. You sacrifice armor pen (no LDR) but you survive and kite better.

## Skill Order

**R > Q > E > W** (revised)

- **Q max first**: On-hit damage (10/20/30/40/50 + 40% AP), AS bonus (20-60%), and spread attack scaling all improve per rank. This is your primary DPS.
- **E second**: MS buff goes from 30% to 50%. During R, this becomes a dash with 45-75% MS. The mobility is more consistently useful than W's damage which you use primarily for the CC, not the damage.
- **W third**: Base damage increases but the 99% slow is the same at all ranks. The slow is what matters for peel/engage, and that's already maxed at rank 1.

**First 3 levels**: Q → E → W (Q for trading/farming, E for mobility, W for CC at level 3)

## Math Appendix

### DPS at 3-item spike (Yun Tal + Fiendhunter + IE)
- Total AD: ~55 base (lvl 11) + 50 (Yun Tal) + 75 (IE) = ~180 AD
- Total AS: 0.65 × (1 + 0.22 + 0.40 + 0.25 + 0.45 + 0.40) = 0.65 × 2.72 = ~1.77 AS (with Q rank 3 active + Yun Tal base AS)
  - With Flurry (30% AS): even higher during fights
- Crit chance: 75% (25 Yun Tal + 25 Fiendhunter + 25 IE)
- Crit damage: 200% + 30% (IE) = 230%
- Per auto: 180 × (0.75 × 2.30 + 0.25 × 1.0) = 180 × (1.725 + 0.25) = 180 × 1.975 = ~355 physical
  - Plus Q on-hit: 30 + 40% AP magic damage (~30 with no AP items)
  - Plus passive: 10% bonus magic damage on crits = ~35 extra per crit
  - Plus spread attacks: each dealing 54 (30% of 180 AD) that can crit

### Why GA doesn't cripple your DPS
- GA gives 55 AD. That's more than Runaan's (0 AD) or LDR (35 AD).
- Q spread with GA: 30% × (180 + 55) = 70.5 per spread (vs 54 without GA)
- W damage with GA: 85% × 55 = 46.75 more damage on W
- You lose the AoE from Runaan's or the armor pen from LDR, but you gain survival + decent AD.
